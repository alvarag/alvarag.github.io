About Me

I received my M.S. degree in Computer Engineering in 2010, and the PhD in Computer Science in 2018 at the University of Burgos for a thesis entitled "Study of Instance Selection Methods".

I am currently an Assistant Professor at the Department of Civil Engineer of the Burgos University. My main research interests include Machine Learning, Data Mining, ensemble classifiers and instance selection.

I am a member of the "Artificial Data Mining Research and Bioinformatics Learning" research group.

Capabilities

Learning and teaching are together.

Language Processors

In this subject the main concepts of Language Processors are taught: such as lexical, syntactical, and semantic analysis, among others. In laboratories, the whole process of a compiler (from lexical analysis to code generation) is put into practice.

Artificial Intelligence

This subject might well be a Master itself, it presents a general view of Artificial Intelligence Probabilistic models, evolutive learning, artificial neural networks, fuzzy logic, among others, are some of the topics that are introduced.

Programming Models for Big Data

In the era of Big Data, new challenges and opportunities emerge for designers and developers around the world. In this subject the main aspects of Big Data are presented, as well as some of the technologies that have grown around it. More specifically, the Apache Spark framework is presented for processing big volumes of information.
